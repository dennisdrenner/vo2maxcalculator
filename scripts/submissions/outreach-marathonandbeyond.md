# Marathon & Beyond outreach

**Target:** marathonandbeyond.com — long-running marathon publication
(originally a print magazine, now a web archive + ongoing content). Their
audience is exactly the kind of distance-running enthusiast who cares
about VO2 max as a training metric.

**Where to find a contact:**
- marathonandbeyond.com — look for "About," "Contact," or editorial contact
- The masthead on a recent article often lists an editor email

## Subject line options

- "Free VO2 max calculator with full percentile chart — fit for your readers?"
- "VO2 max field-test calculator for distance runners — interested in a link?"

## Body (paste-ready, edit as needed)

> Hi [editor name],
>
> Long-time admirer of Marathon & Beyond. I run vo2maxcalculators.com —
> a free, no-signup calculator that implements 17 validated VO2 max
> field tests (Cooper 12-min, 1.5-mile, Rockport walk, Bruce treadmill,
> Norwegian 4x4, etc.) and renders results against the full Cooper
> Institute percentile chart used in ACSM's Guidelines for Exercise
> Testing and Prescription.
>
> The site also has a research-grounded blog covering VO2 max
> longevity science, training protocols, and summaries of what experts
> like Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, Andy Galpin, and Iñigo San-Millán
> say about it.
>
> Two reasons I'm reaching out:
>
> 1. If there's an article in your archive on VO2 max, fitness testing,
>    or marathon training that could benefit from a free public tool
>    in the resources, I'd appreciate a link.
> 2. If you ever commission contributor pieces — I'd be happy to
>    write a properly-sourced feature on field-test accuracy, age-related
>    VO2 max decline, or the science of polarized training. I'm a biology
>    grad (University of Maryland), have undergone CPET testing myself,
>    and have spent years reading the underlying exercise-physiology
>    literature.
>
> Site for reference:
> - https://vo2maxcalculators.com
> - Blog index: https://vo2maxcalculators.com/blog/
>
> Either way, no pressure — and thanks for what M&B has done for the
> distance-running community over the years.
>
> Best,
> Dennis Drenner
> dennis@smartravenai.com

## Notes

- The dual ask (link + contributor pitch) is on purpose. Even if they
  don't link the site, an accepted contributor piece would carry the
  link inside it — typically a stronger placement than a footer link.
- The "I'm a biology grad with CPET experience" credential is the
  thing that makes a cold pitch land. Lead with it.
